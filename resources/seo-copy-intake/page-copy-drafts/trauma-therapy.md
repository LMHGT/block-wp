Trauma therapy in Louisville, KY, may help adults, teens, and families. It may fit when a past event still affects life now. A trauma-informed therapist will work at a pace that fits the person. We do not push clients to tell all the facts at once.

## When This May Help

People can react to hard events in many ways. Trauma therapy may help you or a family member with:

- hard memories that keep coming back.
- avoiding people, places, or events.
- feeling tense, on edge, or quick to react.
- the effects of childhood trauma or medical trauma.
- trauma from past relationships.
- stress that affects home, school, work, or family life.

You do not need to know the right term for what is going on. You can start with what you notice now and what you want help with.

## What Support Can Look Like

Trauma therapy does not always start with the hardest part of the story. Early work may focus on coping skills, safety, trust, and ways to feel more steady. Deeper work can come later if it fits your needs.

The plan may use talk therapy, grounding skills, methods that work on thoughts and actions, family support, or another approach. Adults and teens may work one-on-one through [Individual Counseling](/individual-counseling/). A child may use [Child Therapy](/child-counseling/) with help from a parent or caregiver. A family may also use [Family Therapy](/family-therapy/) when trauma has changed trust, roles, or daily life.

Your therapist will help choose the pace and tools. The plan can change as needs change. No one method is right for every person.

## How Trauma Therapy And EMDR Are Different

Trauma therapy is a broad kind of support. It can include many ways to build skills and work through past events. [EMDR Therapy](/emdr-therapy/) is one trauma-focused option within that larger plan.

EMDR may fit some clients. It is not the only path. Some people start with coping skills or talk therapy. Others may use EMDR later. The choice depends on age, goals, stress, and how ready the person feels for deeper work.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I have to tell the whole story right away?

No. You can start with what is affecting you now. Therapy can first focus on trust, coping skills, safety, and feeling more steady.

### Is EMDR always part of trauma therapy?

No. EMDR is one option. Trauma therapy may also use talk therapy, grounding skills, methods that work on thoughts and actions, child therapy, or family support.

### Can children and teens receive trauma therapy?

Yes, when it fits their needs. The plan may include a parent or caregiver, family sessions, or child-focused tools.

### Does LMHG provide crisis care or medication management?

No. LMHG does not provide emergency crisis care, inpatient or residential care, or medication management. LMHG also does not provide trauma evaluations for court cases or custody opinions.
