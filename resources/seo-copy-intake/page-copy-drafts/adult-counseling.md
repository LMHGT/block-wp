Adult counseling gives you a private place to talk. You can start with what feels hard right now. This may be stress, worry, low mood, grief, anger, trauma, or a major change. At Louisville Mental Health Group, adult therapy is shaped around the person. There is no one plan for every client.

## When This May Help

Adult counseling may help when:

- Stress keeps building
- Worry is hard to turn off
- Low mood gets in the way of daily life
- Grief or past events still feel heavy
- Work stress takes up too much space
- Anger or conflict keeps coming back
- A relationship feels strained
- A life change leaves you feeling stuck

You do not need to know the right therapy term. You can begin by saying what is going on. You can also share what you want to change or understand.

## What Support Can Look Like

Adult counseling is one-on-one therapy. Early talks help the therapist learn what brings you in. You can set goals together. You can also decide what pace feels useful.

Sessions may look at thoughts, feelings, habits, and stress. They may include skills you can use between visits. Some talks may focus on grief or trauma. Others may focus on work, family, or hard choices.

The method should fit the reason you came to therapy. You may meet with one main therapist on a regular basis. If a more focused service may help, another therapist can join the team. For example, a client may also work with a provider who offers [Trauma Therapy](/trauma-therapy/). Each therapist should have a clear role.

LMHG accepts Medicaid, commercial insurance, and private pay options when the plan fits. For more details, see [Costs and Insurance](/insurance/).

## Adult Counseling And Individual Counseling

Adult counseling is a form of [Individual Counseling](/individual-counseling/). It has a clear focus on adult needs. These may include work stress, parenting stress, grief, burnout, or long-term patterns.

Adult counseling is not the same as [Couples Counseling](/couples-counseling/). It is also not family therapy. In adult counseling, one adult has a therapist and a private place to talk. A couple or family service may be added when it would help. A different therapist may lead that work. This helps keep roles and limits clear.

Use the [Reach Out intake form](https://intakeq.com/new/g91Z8x/bjxuno) to share your contact details. Add a short note about the kind of help you want. The form does not ask for clinical details. LMHG will follow up to talk about fit, timing, insurance, and next steps.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is adult counseling private?

Yes. It is one-on-one therapy with privacy limits. Your therapist can explain those limits at the start.

### What can I talk about in adult therapy?

You can talk about stress, worry, low mood, grief, anger, trauma, work, relationships, or life changes.

### Can I work with more than one LMHG therapist?

Sometimes. You may have a main therapist and another therapist for a more focused need. Their roles should stay clear.

### Do I need to choose a therapy method first?

No. Your therapist can help choose methods based on your needs and the reason you reached out.
