# Teen Therapy in Louisville, KY

Teen therapy in Louisville, KY, gives teens a place to talk. It also helps parents make sense of changes at home. A family may reach out when a teen seems sad, worried, angry, shut down, or hard to reach. This kind of adolescent counseling can help the family slow down and learn what the teen needs.

At Louisville Mental Health Group, teen counseling starts with what is happening now. We listen to the teen and the parent. Then we help the family find a plan that fits. The plan may change as needs become clear.

## When This May Help

Teen therapy may help when a teen has:

- Mood changes, sadness, or anger
- Worry, panic, stress, or fear
- School stress or trouble keeping up
- Social stress or friendship problems
- More time alone or less interest in things
- Family conflict or trouble talking at home
- Grief, trauma, or a major life change

Parents may see a change but not know why it began. Teens may also know that something feels wrong but have a hard time saying it. You do not need to know the cause before you reach out.

Some families are not sure where to start. [Child Therapy](/child-counseling/) is often for kids ages 2 to 12. Teen therapy is made for the needs of teens and their families.

## What Support Can Look Like

Teen therapy often includes time for the teen to meet with the therapist alone. Parents or caregivers may also join check-ins or family sessions. At the first visit, the therapist explains what can stay private. The therapist also explains what may be shared and when a safety concern or law requires sharing. When possible, the therapist talks with the teen before sharing and shares only what is needed.

LMHG may use a team approach when it helps. A teen may have one main therapist. Another therapist may join if a special skill is needed. Each role should stay clear. For some older teens, [Individual Counseling](/individual-counseling/) may be a better fit.

## Teen Therapy, Child Therapy, And Family Work

Teen therapy gives teens more say in the work. It also gives them more room to talk alone. In child therapy, parents are often more involved. This is more common with young children.

Parents can still be part of teen therapy. They may share what has changed. They may help with home routines. They may also join a session when it makes sense.

At times, the main issue affects the whole home. In that case, [Family Therapy](/family-therapy/) may be added. It may also be the best place to start. The family and therapist can decide this together.

## FAQs

### Is teen therapy only for serious problems?

No. It can help with stress, worry, sadness, school pressure, social strain, family conflict, or a hard change.

### Will parents be part of teen therapy?

They may be. Parent check-ins or family sessions can be used when they fit the teen's needs.

### Can a teen have privacy in therapy?

Yes, but privacy has limits. At the first visit, the therapist explains what may be shared and when a safety concern or law requires sharing.

### Is teen therapy the same as family therapy?

No. Teen therapy centers on the teen. Family therapy works more on patterns and stress across the family.
