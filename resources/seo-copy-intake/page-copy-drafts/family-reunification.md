# Family Reunification Services in Louisville, KY

At LMHG, Family Reunification is a paced service. It helps after a break in a parent-child bond. The work is impartial. It is a court-related service. It is not regular therapy or counseling.

Either parent may start. A custodial parent cares for the child each day. A reunifying parent has had a break in contact or trust.

## When This May Help

Family Reunification Services may fit when a clear court order calls for help with:

- a long gap in parent-child contact.
- a custody change.
- conflict between a parent and child.
- CPS involvement.
- family separation or instability.
- trust and contact after an attachment break.
- a need for safe, structured pacing.

Goals may include safe contact, clear roles, trust, and better talks. Progress is not promised. The pace must fit the child. It must also fit the case history and court order.

Each case has its own facts. The provider sets a structured pace. The child, case history, safety needs, and court order all matter. Contact may start in small steps. The plan can change as needs shift.

## What Support Can Look Like

LMHG first learns the case history. It then reviews the court order. The order must name this service the right way. Both parties must agree to work with LMHG. Both must sign the reunification agreement.

The provider will choose which party to meet first. Case history, contact, and safety needs may guide that choice. The work then moves at a set pace. The provider stays impartial. They do not take sides.

The work may include:

- documentation of the service.
- recommendations from the provider.
- testimony when it is right for the case and role.

Not every case calls for these steps.

## How Family Reunification Differs From Therapy

Some people search for reunification therapy. At LMHG, this service is not regular therapy or counseling. [Family therapy](/family-therapy/) is broader work on family ties, stress, and patterns. It does not replace this court-based process.

[Co-Parenting Services](/co-parenting/) may fit shared choices across two homes. The [Court-Ordered Services](/court-ordered/) page shows how these services relate.

This service is not a custody evaluation or mediation. It does not include supervised visitation. It is not an emergency crisis service. LMHG does not give legal advice. LMHG cannot take a case without an accurately worded court order. The provider does not promise a court result.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Family Reunification the same as reunification therapy?

No. People may use that search term. LMHG treats Family Reunification as its own impartial, court-related service.

### Which parent can contact LMHG?

Either parent can reach out. This may be the custodial parent. It may also be the parent who seeks to rebuild contact.

### What is needed before the service can start?

LMHG must review an accurately worded court order. Both parties must agree to the service. Both must sign the reunification agreement.

### Will the provider decide custody or take sides?

No. The provider is impartial. They do not perform custody evaluations. They may give recommendations or testimony when the case and role call for them.
