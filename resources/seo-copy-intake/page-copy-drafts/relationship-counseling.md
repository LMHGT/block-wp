Relationship counseling helps partners see what is going on between them. It may focus on trust, closeness, patterns that repeat, and hard choices. Louisville Mental Health Group works with married and unmarried couples. This includes same-sex and opposite-sex couples.

## When This May Help

Relationship counseling may help when:

- you do not feel as close as you once did.
- trust has been hurt or is hard to build again.
- talks end in blame, silence, or more doubt.
- the same roles or habits keep causing stress.
- parenting, money, sex, family, or life changes put stress on the bond.
- one or both partners do not know what they want next.
- you want a clear way to talk about staying together, separating, or making a major choice.

You do not need to agree on each goal before the first visit. The first step may be to learn what each person wants to know or change.

## What Support Can Look Like

A therapist can give hard talks a clear structure. Sessions may help each partner state their needs and hear the other person's view. They may also help the couple spot a pattern that keeps taking over. The work may cover trust, affection, and shared tasks. It may also cover limits with other people and hard choices at home.

The therapist does not take sides or tell a couple what to choose. Each plan should fit the couple and why they asked for help. Counseling may help partners get clear about their needs. It cannot promise that the bond will heal or that a set outcome will occur.

One or both partners may also see a separate [individual therapist](/individual-counseling/). LMHG may use more than one therapist when that helps. Each role and privacy limit must stay clear.

## Relationship Counseling And Other Couples Services

[Couples counseling](/couples-counseling/) is a broad term for therapy with married or unmarried partners. It can overlap with relationship counseling and marriage counseling. This page puts more focus on trust, closeness, patterns that repeat, and choices about the relationship.

[Conflict resolution counseling](/couples-conflict-resolution/) has a tighter focus on fights that get worse and on shutting down. It also covers repair after a fight. A couple may have needs that fit both areas. LMHG can help choose the best page or provider to start with.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is relationship counseling the same as couples or marriage counseling?

The terms often overlap. LMHG works with married and unmarried couples. The best label matters less than why you want help.

### Do we need to know if we want to stay together?

No. Counseling can give both partners a clear place to talk about the relationship and what each person wants next.

### Can we each have our own therapist?

Yes. One or both partners may have their own therapist. Clear roles help protect privacy and keep the couples work on track.

### Will the therapist take sides or fix the relationship?

No. The therapist does not choose a winner. No outcome is promised. LMHG does not give legal or custody opinions through this service.
