# Parent-Child Attachment Therapy in Louisville, KY

At LMHG, parent-child attachment therapy helps a child and parent rebuild trust. It may fit after stress, time apart, loss, moves, or other hard change. The work looks at the bond between a child and parent.

This work often fits within [family therapy](/family-therapy/). It may also connect with [child therapy](/child-counseling/) when the child needs their own support.

## When This May Help

You may be looking for help because:

- A child pulls away or shuts down with a parent.
- A child seems guarded or does not ask for comfort.
- A parent is not sure how to respond to big feelings.
- Trust has been hurt by stress, loss, or time apart.
- A move or other family change has been hard.
- Daily routines lead to fear or power struggles.

These signs do not tell us the cause on their own. A therapist can help the family look at the full picture and choose a clear plan.

## What Support Can Look Like

Sessions may include the child, the parent, the family, or a mix. The plan will depend on the child's age, family history, safety needs, and current stress.

The therapist may help the parent spot patterns and slow down hard moments. Parents can learn ways to stay calm, set clear roles, and respond in ways the child can grasp. The child may get help naming feelings and making up after conflict.

The work may cover:

- Calm home routines.
- Repair after an argument.
- Clear roles for adults and children.
- Simple ways to talk about hard topics.
- Parent coaching and check-ins.

Some families need help with one short-term strain. Others need a slower plan. LMHG does not assume one provider can meet every need. Another provider may help when a child or parent needs a different type of therapy.

## How This Fits With Family And Child Therapy

Family therapy is broad. It may focus on conflict, routines, stress, or how family members talk with each other. Attachment therapy has a tighter focus on trust between a child and parent.

Child therapy gives a child their own place to work on feelings, behavior, stress, or trauma. When the main concern is the parent-child bond, the parent will often take part too.

LMHG must review court-related family needs before naming the right service. Attachment therapy is not a custody review, legal opinion, or promise that a family will reunite.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is this page about adult attachment styles?

No. At LMHG, this service is only for trust and repair between a child and parent. LMHG is not adding a separate adult attachment page at this time.

### Does a parent take part?

Often, yes. Parent involvement matters when the main concern is the bond between the child and parent.

### Can this help after a family change?

It may fit when loss, time apart, a move, or another change has hurt trust. LMHG will review the details first.

### Is this the same as family therapy?

It may be part of family therapy, but it has a tighter focus on parent-child trust and repair.
