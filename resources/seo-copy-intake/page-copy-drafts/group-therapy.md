# Group Therapy in Louisville, KY

Group therapy in Louisville, KY, at LMHG is led by a clinician. This clinician-led group therapy gives clients a set place to talk, learn, and practice skills with other people. It can offer a different kind of help than one-on-one or family therapy. For some clients, it can be one part of a larger plan.

Group topics and open spots change. LMHG currently has a teen-focused group. Its full description will be added here when it is ready. This does not mean the group has an open spot. Ask about current therapy groups in Louisville, KY, before you plan to join one.

## When This May Help

Group therapy may help a client who wants to:

- learn with people who share a common concern;
- hear how others handle a hard choice or feeling;
- practice a skill during a planned session;
- feel less alone while working on a goal;
- give and receive help with clear group rules; or
- add a new kind of learning to other therapy.

The group topic, age range, time, goals, and open spots all matter. A clinician must check that the group fits the client. A group is not the right first step for each person.

## What Support Can Look Like

The group leader sets the plan for each session. They explain the rules and help people stay on the topic. A session may have a short lesson, a guided talk, skill practice, or time to think. The exact plan depends on the group.

Clients may learn from one another and help each other. The clinician still leads the work. Groups are not peer-led, anonymous, or drop-in. Group therapy does not give emergency help.

The clinician also explains privacy rules and their limits. Group members are expected to respect each other's privacy, but LMHG cannot promise what another member may share outside the group.

## How Group Therapy Differs From Other Therapy

Group therapy includes several clients and one or more clinicians. The shared setting is part of the work. [Individual Counseling](/individual-counseling/) gives one client more one-on-one time with a therapist. [Family Therapy](/family-therapy/) brings family members into therapy to work on shared needs.

Group therapy can add a different kind of practice and shared learning to individual or family therapy. A clinician can help decide whether a group is a useful part of the client's plan.

Group details can change. LMHG will explain the topic, schedule, age range, and any other steps before a client joins.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Who leads group therapy at LMHG?

An LMHG clinician leads each group. The clinician sets the plan, guides the talk, and explains the rules.

### Is a group open right now?

Open groups and spots change. Ask LMHG what is open now. Full group details will be posted when they are ready.

### Can group therapy replace individual therapy?

Group therapy can be a useful addition to individual or family therapy. Fit depends on the client's needs and the group.

### Is group therapy for crisis help?

No. Group therapy is planned care. It does not provide emergency or 24-hour help.
