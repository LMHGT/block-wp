Anxiety and depression therapy gives you a place to talk. It can help you look at worry, low mood, stress, and daily strain. Louisville Mental Health Group provides therapy and counseling. LMHG does not provide psychiatry or medication management.

## When This May Help

Anxiety and depression can look different from one person to the next. You may want help when you notice:

- Thoughts that race or will not slow down
- Worry, fear, or panic
- Low mood or a numb feeling
- Low drive or less interest in things
- Sleep changes or feeling worn down
- Avoiding people, places, school, or work
- Stress that hurts daily routines
- Grief, family stress, or a major change

You do not need to know the cause before you reach out. You also do not need a diagnosis. You can start with what you notice. Then you and a therapist can talk about what may fit.

## What Support Can Look Like

Therapy may help you spot patterns and triggers. It may also help you build coping skills. You and the therapist can make a plan for daily life. The plan should fit your needs and goals.

Sessions may look at thoughts, feelings, habits, and stress. They may also look at sleep, school, work, or home life. The pace should make sense for you. You do not have to tell every hard detail at once.

Anxiety therapy and depression counseling are often part of [Individual Counseling](/individual-counseling/). Some clients work with one therapist. Others may use LMHG's team approach. If past events are a main concern, [Trauma Therapy](/trauma-therapy/) may also fit.

## Therapy, Psychiatry, And Medication Care

Therapy and medication care have different roles. LMHG provides therapy and counseling. We do not prescribe or manage medicine. We also do not replace a medical provider.

Some people use therapy and medication care at the same time. The services come from separate providers. A therapist can work on coping skills, patterns, goals, and daily plans. A medical or psychiatry provider should answer questions about medicine.

This page is about LMHG therapy. It does not give medical advice. It also does not promise that symptoms will change.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does LMHG provide psychiatry or medication management?

No. LMHG provides anxiety therapy and depression counseling. We do not prescribe or manage medicine.

### Can therapy be used with medication care?

Yes. Some clients use both through separate providers. Medicine questions should go to a medical or psychiatry provider.

### Do I need a diagnosis before reaching out?

No. You can start with what you notice, such as worry, low mood, panic, stress, or feeling stuck.

### Does therapy guarantee that symptoms will improve?

No. Therapy may help you learn about patterns and build skills. No change or result can be promised.
