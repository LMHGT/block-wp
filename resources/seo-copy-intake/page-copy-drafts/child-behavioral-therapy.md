# Child Behavioral Therapy in Louisville, KY

Child behavioral therapy helps parents make sense of behavior they see at home or school. The cause may not be clear yet. A therapist looks at what happens before, during, and after the behavior. Then the family can build a plan that fits the child.

This service is often used with children ages 2 to 12. It is one type of [child therapy](/child-counseling/), not a one-size plan for every child.

## When This May Help

This service may help when a child has:

- Big reactions that are hard to calm.
- Hitting, yelling, running off, or frequent refusal.
- School calls or trouble with class rules.
- Hard mornings, bedtimes, or screen limits.
- Meltdowns or shutdowns after a change.
- Stress after grief, trauma, a move, or family conflict.
- Behavior that leaves parents unsure what to try next.

Parents do not need to know why the behavior is happening before they reach out. Starting with what you see is enough.

## What Support Can Look Like

The plan may include time with the child, parent check-ins, family sessions, or help with home routines. School contact may also be part of the plan when it is useful and allowed.

The child may learn to name feelings, calm their body, and recover after conflict. Parents may learn how to use clear limits and steady follow-through. Younger children often need more parent help. Older children may spend more time alone with the therapist.

LMHG does not use the same plan for every child. Some children need more play-based work. Some need more work on routines and skills. [Family therapy](/family-therapy/) may help when stress involves more than one person at home.

This service does not include psychiatry or medicine. LMHG does not provide autism or learning tests. We also do not provide ABA, speech therapy, or occupational therapy. This service does not include custody opinions or crisis response.

## How This Differs From Child Therapy And Play Therapy

Child therapy is the broad parent service. It can help with feelings, grief, trauma, school stress, behavior, or family change.

Child behavioral therapy has a closer focus on behavior that affects daily life. It looks at patterns, triggers, routines, skills, and parent response.

[Play therapy](/play-therapy/) uses play and other tools that fit the child's age. It can help a child show feelings that are hard to put into words. Child behavioral work may use play too, but its main focus stays on behavior patterns and daily skills.
