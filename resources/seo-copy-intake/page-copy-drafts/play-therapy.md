# Play Therapy in Louisville, KY

Play therapy is a type of therapy for children. It helps a child show feelings that may be hard to explain with words. A therapist may use toys, art, stories, and games with a clear goal. It is not just free play, a reward, or a way to keep a child busy.

At LMHG, play therapy may fit a child who needs help with feelings, behavior, stress, grief, trauma, school, or family change. Parents take part in a way that fits the child. Younger children often need more parent help.

## When This May Help

Play therapy may help when a child has:

- Big feelings that come out fast.
- Meltdowns, shutdowns, or strong fear.
- Trouble with sleep, school, or daily routines.
- More anger, worry, or sadness.
- Stress after loss, a move, divorce, or family conflict.
- Hard behavior at home or school.
- Trouble talking about something that happened.

Parents often know something feels wrong before they know why. Starting with what you see is enough.

## What Support Can Look Like

The therapist pays close attention to the child's play. They may notice themes, choices, stress, and how the child responds to limits. Play can help the child name feelings, build trust, practice skills, or work through hard events at the child's pace.

Parent support will fit the child and family. It may include parent check-ins, help with home routines, family sessions, school contact, or ideas to try between visits. Younger children will often need more parent help than older children.

Some families need more than one type of support. The team can help decide which service is the best place to start.

## How Play Therapy Is Different From Other Child Services

Play therapy is one type of [child therapy](/child-counseling/). Child therapy is the broad service. It may include talk, play, parent help, family work, school help, or coping skills.

[Child behavioral therapy](/child-behavioral-intervention/) has a closer focus on behavior patterns, triggers, routines, and daily skills. It may fit when the main concern is repeated behavior at home or school.

Play therapy may fit when a child needs a way to show feelings through play. These paths can overlap. The right start depends on the child's age, what is happening, and what the family needs.

[Family therapy](/family-therapy/) may help when the main concern is a pattern between family members.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What ages is play therapy for?

It is most often used with younger children. Fit depends on the child, the concern, and the provider.

### How involved are parents?

Parent involvement is based on the child. Younger children often need more parent help.

### How is play therapy different from ordinary play?

Play therapy has a therapy goal. The therapist uses play to learn about needs and help the child practice skills.
