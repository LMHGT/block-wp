# In-Home Mental Health Therapy in Louisville, KY

In-home mental health therapy is a therapy visit held in the client's home. It is not home health care or physical therapy. It is also not a separate type of therapy. The therapist still picks an approach. It is based on the client's needs and goals. The home is simply where the visit takes place.

For some clients and families, meeting at home can help. The therapist can learn about home routines and stress. The family can talk about needs as they come up in daily life. In-home therapy is offered only when the setting is a good fit.

## When This May Help

In-home therapy may be worth asking about when:

- travel makes office visits hard.
- home routines are a main part of the concern.
- family stress is easier to understand at home.
- a child has needs that show up most in the home.
- the therapist needs to see how a daily pattern works.
- office-only care does not show the full picture.

An in-home visit is not right for every person or concern. Fit depends on the service and the client's needs. Safety, location, insurance, and an open provider also matter.

## What Support Can Look Like

An in-home session is a planned therapy visit. It is still mental health care. The therapist may meet with one person. Family may join when that is part of the plan. A session may focus on feelings, behavior, talking, coping skills, or home routines. The therapist and client choose what makes sense.

The plan should fit the client. Some people may use home visits for part of their care. They may use office or online visits at other times. Others may find that [family therapy](/family-therapy/) or another service fits better.

Commercial insurance or Medicaid may cover in-home therapy. Coverage is not promised. LMHG must check the plan, cost, service fit, and open providers. The [costs and insurance page](/insurance/) gives general payment details.

## In-Home Therapy and Community Support Are Different

In-home therapy is therapy from a therapist in the home. [Community Support](/community-support/) is a separate service. An associate helps a client practice therapy goals. This may happen in the community. The associate also works with the client's regular therapist.

The main difference is the job, not just the place. A therapist gives therapy. A Community Support Associate helps with practice outside therapy. LMHG may use more than one provider when that fits the plan.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is in-home therapy the same as home health or physical therapy?

No. LMHG provides mental health therapy in the home. It does not provide home health care or physical therapy.

### Is in-home therapy available to everyone?

No. Need, fit, safety, location, insurance, and open provider times all matter.

### Does insurance cover in-home therapy?

Commercial insurance or Medicaid may cover it. LMHG must check the plan and service before care starts.

### Can family members join an in-home session?

They may join when the therapist and client agree that family input fits the therapy plan.
