# Community Support Services in Louisville, KY

Community support services in Louisville, KY, help clients practice therapy goals in daily life. These mental health community support services are led by a Community Support Associate, or CSA. The CSA works with the client and stays in touch with the client's regular therapist. The client still meets with that therapist for therapy.

This is a mental health service. It is not crisis care or a general social service. It helps clients use skills outside the therapy room. This is where daily stress and real choices happen.

## When This May Help

Community support may help when a client understands a skill in therapy but has a hard time using it day to day. Support is based on the client's own goals and plan. It may include help with:

- building a steady daily routine.
- using coping skills during stress.
- practicing clear communication.
- speaking up for their needs.
- breaking a large goal into smaller steps.
- following through on goals set with a therapist.

Community support is often short-term and focused. The type and amount of help can look different for each client.

## What Support Can Look Like

The therapist, client, and CSA work as a team. The therapist guides the therapy plan. The CSA helps the client practice parts of that plan. The work takes place in daily settings when it is a good fit. The team may review what worked. They may also talk about what felt hard and what to try next.

Practice may happen while the client works through a routine, prepares for an appointment, uses a coping skill, or speaks up in a real setting. The activity should connect to a therapy goal. It is not just time spent together in the community.

This team plan gives each provider a clear job. It also keeps therapy and skill practice tied together. Community support does not replace regular therapy.

Community support is mostly Medicaid-based. A private-pay or fee-for-service plan may be possible case by case. LMHG must check cost, fit, and service needs before care starts. The [costs and insurance page](/insurance/) explains general payment options.

## Community Support and Case Management Are Different

Community support focuses on therapy goals in real life. [Case Management](/case-management/) has a different job. It helps with plans, basic needs, and resources. It can also link providers, schools, insurance plans, or community groups.

Some clients may need one service. Others may need both. LMHG can also explain other [community-based mental health services](/community-based-services/) when the right first step is not clear.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is community support the same as therapy?

No. A CSA helps a client practice therapy goals in daily life. The client still sees a regular therapist.

### Is community support only for Medicaid clients?

It is mostly Medicaid-based. A private-pay plan may be possible case by case.

### Where does community support happen?

It may happen in a community setting that fits the client's plan. Location and provider availability must be checked first.

### Is community support a crisis service?

No. It does not provide emergency or 24-hour help.
