# Community-Based Mental Health Services in Louisville, KY

Community-based mental health services in Louisville, KY, can help when daily needs make therapy harder. LMHG can help clients plan next steps and connect with resources. The work can link you to help, teach skills, and help you act on a plan. It is often short and has a clear goal.

This page explains how case management in Louisville, KY, and community support services in Louisville, KY, fit with therapy. Each service has its own role. A person may need one service or a small team.

LMHG is a small local practice. We can offer practical help that is often found only at a large agency. We look at the whole person, not just what happens in a therapy visit.

## When This May Help

This kind of help may fit when daily needs make it hard to focus in therapy. It may include:

- helping each provider use the same plan;
- finding help for food, housing, or other basic needs;
- learning to ask questions and speak up for your needs;
- making a crisis plan before a hard time becomes an emergency;
- getting links to local help;
- planning for a school meeting or Individualized Education Program, often called an IEP; or
- following through on calls, forms, and next steps.

LMHG cannot promise that a resource will be open. These services do not provide emergency help, rides, medicine, inpatient care, or a place to live.

## What Support Can Look Like

The first step is to learn what is in the way. A team member may help set a short list of goals. They may link you to a service or plan for a meeting. They can also help you speak up. With your consent, they may work with a therapist, doctor, school, or other group.

Case Management and Community Support are most often paid for by Medicaid. Private pay may be an option for people with other plans. Many plans cover in-home therapy, but we must check. A fee may apply to school meetings if a plan will not pay for them.

## How Community-Based Services Fit Together

This page is a guide to three types of help. [Case Management](/case-management/) helps with plans, basic needs, links to help, and work between providers.

[Community Support Services](/community-support/) help a client use therapy goals in daily life. A Community Support Associate works with the client and stays in touch with the therapist.

[In-Home Therapy](/locations/in-home/) is therapy held in the home. The home is the place for the visit. It is not the same as Case Management or Community Support.

Some people need one type of help. Others may need a team with clear roles. LMHG can help you pick a first step.

## Getting Started

Use the [Reach Out form](https://intakeq.com/new/g91Z8x/bjxuno). Share your name and a short note about the help you need. You do not need to pick the right service first. The form does not ask for health details. Someone from LMHG will check fit, cost, and provider availability.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are community-based services the same as therapy?

No. They add hands-on help to therapy. In-home therapy is different. It is a therapy visit held at home.

### Are these services only for people with Medicaid?

No. Medicaid often pays for Case Management and Community Support. Private pay may be an option for other clients.

### Can LMHG attend an IEP meeting?

School support may be available. A fee may apply when insurance does not pay for the meeting.

### Do these services include emergency help?

No. A team member may help make a crisis plan, but LMHG does not offer emergency or 24-hour help.
