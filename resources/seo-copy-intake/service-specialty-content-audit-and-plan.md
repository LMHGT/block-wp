## Content Standard

Each promoted page should meet all of these rules:

1. 500 to 700 page-specific visible words, including page-specific FAQs but excluding navigation, footer, and generic related-page cards.
2. Approximate Flesch-Kincaid grade 6.0 or lower.
3. One clear search intent, one primary phrase, and supporting terms that do not belong to a child page.
4. A plain H1 that names the service and Louisville when it reads naturally.
5. A short opening that tells the reader what the service is and who it may help.
6. Scannable H2 sections, short paragraphs, and a useful unordered list where examples help.
7. Accurate service limits, insurance language, age guidance, and court or safety boundaries.
8. Two to five useful internal links with descriptive anchor text. Repeating a relevant destination in navigation and once in page copy is acceptable. Avoid repeating the same body link without a new reason.
9. Three to five page-specific FAQs based on real intake questions.
10. A Reach Out CTA that leads to the intake form and does not ask for clinical details.
11. A unique title tag, meta description, canonical URL, and indexable sitemap entry.

## Keyword Ownership

Use these boundaries to reduce cannibalization:

| Page family | Parent owns | Child pages own |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Individual | broad one-to-one counseling, privacy, customization | Adult owns adult-life intent; Anxiety and Depression owns symptom-specific therapy intent |
| Child | broad parent-led child therapy and choosing an approach | Teen owns teen concerns; Play owns play therapy; Child Behavioral owns behavior-focused therapy |
| Family | broad family patterns, communication, and added family sessions | Attachment owns parent-child attachment; Parenting owns parent skill support; Conflict Resolution owns repeated conflict |
| Couples | two-partner counseling, relationship counseling, marriage counseling, hard conversations | Conflict Resolution is linked for non-couple family relationships and belongs under Family |
| Court | court-ordered therapy and court-related service intake | Co-Parenting owns co-parenting intervention; Reunification owns the distinct reunification service |
| Community | wraparound philosophy and practical service access | Case Management owns case management terms; Community Support owns CSA and therapy-goal practice |
| Trauma | broad trauma therapy and symptom-based search intent | EMDR owns EMDR and secondary PTSD treatment intent |

The Community-Based Services draft should not target the exact primary phrases assigned to Case Management and Community Support. Play Therapy should not target Child Behavioral Therapy as a secondary keyword.
